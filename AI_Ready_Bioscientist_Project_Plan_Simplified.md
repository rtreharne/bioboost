# BioBoost: AI Skills for Bioscientists

**Author:** R. Treharne
**Date:** 2 June 2026

## 1. Project Overview

### Proposal

Develop a dedicated self-enrolment Canvas course for all undergraduate and postgraduate taught students in the School of Biosciences.

The course will help students use generative AI effectively, critically and responsibly. It will focus on practical strategies for learning, revision and employability, while reinforcing the importance of academic integrity, data protection and independent judgement.

The course will be available to students throughout the academic year and promoted through every taught module's Canvas space.

### Overall Aim

To equip Biosciences students with practical and transferable AI skills that support academic learning and prepare them for an AI-enabled workplace.

### Objectives

BioBoost will:

* provide an accessible introduction to generative AI for students with different levels of prior experience
* show students how to use AI to develop understanding rather than simply obtain answers
* teach practical AI-supported revision strategies, including retrieval practice, quizzes, mock vivas and misconception checks
* develop students' ability to question, verify and improve AI-generated outputs
* establish clear expectations for safe, responsible and transparent AI use
* reinforce existing academic integrity guidance
* connect AI skills with employability through practical career-focused activities
* provide an optional advanced route into AI-assisted data exploration and visualisation

### How It Will Work

1. A central BioBoost course will be created in Canvas.
2. Students will self-enrol using a simple course link.
3. Every taught module will signpost students to the course from its Canvas space.
4. Students will complete a short foundation pathway made up of Canvas modules, practical examples and knowledge checks.
5. Students will complete a final automated viva using the MachinaViva platform.
6. Students who complete the pathway will receive the BioBoost Award.
7. An optional advanced distinction will be piloted separately for students interested in AI-assisted data analysis.

### Key Features

| Element         | Description                                                                                  |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Delivery        | A self-paced Canvas course available through self-enrolment.                                 |
| Audience        | All undergraduate and postgraduate taught Biosciences students.                              |
| Duration        | Approximately two to three hours for the foundation pathway.                                 |
| Content         | Learning, revision, critical evaluation, responsible use and employability.                  |
| Completion      | Short knowledge checks and a final MachinaViva challenge.                                    |
| Recognition     | BioBoost Award, named certificate and reusable CV and LinkedIn wording.                      |
| Advanced option | AI-Enabled Data Explorer Distinction using GitHub Copilot agent mode.                        |
| Cost            | The core pathway will use free-to-access platforms and will not require a paid subscription. |

### Initial Delivery Target

Launch the foundation pathway in **September 2026** as an optional School-wide offer.

The advanced pathway will be developed and piloted later in the academic year so that it does not delay the main launch.

## 2. Impact

### Impact Statement

BioBoost will give Biosciences students a practical, accessible route to using generative AI with confidence, judgement and integrity. By embedding short, self-paced training in Canvas, the project will reach students across programmes without creating a financial barrier. Students will learn how to use AI to deepen understanding, revise actively, check unreliable outputs and communicate their skills to employers. The optional Data Explorer Distinction will provide a clear progression route into AI-assisted scientific data analysis. The project will help the School respond constructively to rapid technological change while strengthening student learning, digital confidence, employability and responsible professional practice across the School.

### Intended Impact

BioBoost will:

* give students a constructive and practical framework for using AI
* reduce passive or inappropriate reliance on AI-generated answers
* improve students' ability to evaluate unreliable AI outputs
* support active revision and independent learning
* strengthen awareness of academic integrity and data protection
* give students a clear way to describe their AI skills to employers
* provide a route into more advanced AI-assisted scientific workflows
* create a consistent School-level response to generative AI

## 3. Foundation Pathway

### Working Title

**BioBoost: AI Skills for Bioscientists**

### Audience

All undergraduate and postgraduate taught students in the School of Biosciences.

### Format

The foundation pathway will be delivered as a short, self-paced Canvas course.

Each module will include:

* a short explanation
* practical examples
* one or more reusable prompts
* a short activity
* a knowledge check that must be passed before the next module unlocks

Students will be able to retry knowledge checks where necessary.

### Course Content

| Module                  | Focus                                   | Examples                                                                                              | Indicative Time |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------: |
| 1. Use AI responsibly   | Safe and appropriate use                | Academic integrity, data protection, disclosure, module-specific rules and links to existing guidance |          20 min |
| 2. Understand the tools | What generative AI can and cannot do    | Capabilities, limitations, hallucinations and the need for human judgement                            |          15 min |
| 3. Learn smarter        | Use AI to understand course material    | Alternative explanations, glossaries, comparisons, worked examples and Socratic questioning           |          25 min |
| 4. Revise actively      | Use AI for active revision              | Retrieval practice, quizzes, short-answer questions, mock vivas and revision planning                 |          25 min |
| 5. Check the output     | Evaluate AI-generated content           | Fact-checking, fabricated references, oversimplification, bias and weak reasoning                     |          25 min |
| 6. Prepare for work     | Apply AI skills to employability        | Job descriptions, CV evidence, interview practice and professional communication                      |          25 min |
| Final challenge         | Demonstrate understanding and judgement | Automated MachinaViva conversation, transcript submission and feedback review                         |          20 min |

**Expected total completion time:** approximately two to three hours.

### Completion and Recognition

To receive the BioBoost Award, students must:

1. complete all six Canvas modules
2. pass the knowledge check at the end of each module
3. complete the final MachinaViva challenge
4. submit their MachinaViva transcript and review the feedback provided

Students who complete the course will receive:

* the **BioBoost Award**
* a named completion certificate
* suggested CV wording
* suggested LinkedIn wording
* a downloadable prompt toolkit for learning and revision

### Suggested CV Wording

> Completed BioBoost: AI Skills for Bioscientists through the University of Liverpool School of Biosciences. Developed practical skills in responsible AI use, academic integrity, critical evaluation of AI-generated outputs, AI-supported revision and professional communication.

### Suggested LinkedIn Wording

> I have completed BioBoost: AI Skills for Bioscientists through the School of Biosciences at the University of Liverpool. The course focused on responsible and transparent use of generative AI, critical evaluation of AI-generated outputs, academic integrity, and practical strategies for learning, revision and employability.

### Student-Facing Message

> Use AI to strengthen your learning, not replace it. Learn how to question the output, protect your data and develop skills that you can explain and defend in academic and professional settings.

## 4. Optional Advanced Pathway

### AI-Enabled Data Explorer Distinction

The AI-Enabled Data Explorer Distinction will provide an optional extension for students who want to develop more advanced technical and employability-focused skills.

It will not be required for the foundation BioBoost Award.

### Purpose

To introduce students to agent-based AI for scientific data exploration, analysis and visualisation.

Students will use GitHub Copilot agent mode in Visual Studio Code to analyse a small open dataset and produce a simple, reproducible portfolio project.

### Indicative Time

Approximately three to five hours.

### Proposed Activity

Students will:

* work with a small open, anonymised or synthetic bioscience dataset
* use GitHub Copilot agent mode to inspect the dataset
* identify one biologically meaningful question
* create a simple R or Python analysis
* produce at least one clear scientific visualisation
* run the code and respond to errors
* check whether the output is scientifically meaningful
* document how AI was used
* explain what was checked manually
* identify where human judgement was required
* submit a small GitHub portfolio project

### Safeguards

The activity will:

* use only open, anonymised or synthetic teaching data
* prohibit the upload of personal, sensitive or unpublished data
* emphasise verification rather than passive acceptance of AI-generated code
* be designed to work within free access limits
* remain separate from assessed coursework unless specifically approved

### Recognition

Students completing the extension will receive:

**BioBoost Award with AI-Enabled Data Explorer Distinction**

## 7. Main Risks and Controls

| Risk                                           | Control                                                                           |
| ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The course becomes too long or complicated     | Keep the foundation pathway concise. Treat additional material as optional.       |
| Students see the course as compliance training | Focus on practical benefits, employability and useful activities.                 |
| Students enrol but do not complete the course  | Use short modules, visible progress, clear recognition and manageable activities. |
| AI tools change quickly                        | Focus on transferable skills and review platform-specific guidance regularly.     |
| Students upload inappropriate data             | Use clear safeguards and teaching datasets in all activities.                     |
| The advanced pathway delays the main launch    | Develop and pilot it separately after the foundation course is available.         |
| Free platform access changes                   | Keep the core pathway platform-flexible and review external tools before launch.  |

## 8. Success Measures

### Foundation Pathway

The first-semester success measures are:

* the Canvas course is available through self-enrolment to all Biosciences students
* every taught module signposts students to BioBoost
* at least 20% of School students enrol during the first semester
* Canvas analytics show progression through modules and completion of knowledge checks
* at least 60% of enrolled students complete the core pathway and final challenge
* at least 80% of student respondents describe the course as clear, useful and manageable
* at least 75% of respondents report increased confidence in using AI for learning and revision

### Advanced Distinction

The initial success measures are:

* a small pilot group completes the Data Explorer activity
* students produce a reproducible data analysis and scientific visualisation
* students can explain how AI was used and how the output was checked
* feedback is gathered before wider promotion
